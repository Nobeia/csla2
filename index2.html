CSLA

As a registered non-profit organization, the Canadian Sustainable Leisure Association (CSLA) is committed to enhancing the quality of life for individuals and families, creating a stronger, more connected, and environmentally conscious society while supporting sustainable leisure and cultural exchange.

Our diverse and capable membership is made up of social groups, social impact firms and passionate individuals.

About CSLA

VISION

Newcomers and diverse communities can thrive through sustainable leisure, cultural connection, and lifelong learning. By fostering wellness, environmental stewardship, and community engagement, we aim to build a more inclusive, fulfilling, and sustainable future for all.

MISSION

Through a combination of recreational activities, cultural education, and community-building initiatives, we dedicated to empowering new immigrants and community members by creating opportunities for connection, personal growth, and environmental responsibility.

Our Members

A CSLA member is an organization, group, private entity or individual based in Canada who promotes an understanding of sustainable development.

As a growing and thriving network, CSLA actively seeks new members and partnerships that align with our mission of engaging our members and others to share knowledge, build relationships, and develop their capacity towards achieving sustainable global development.

Individual Membership

To qualify for individual membership with CSLA, an applicant must:

- Be a person interested in sustainable leisure development and the work of CSLA

Further, the applicant must also:

- Adhere to CSLA’s constitution, mission statement and bylaws

- Acknowledge that the only spokespersons for CSLA is the CSLA Board of Directors, CSLA Executive Director or a CSLA designated spokesperson

- Pay an annual membership fee of $100, or $50 for students and low-income individuals

* Individual members do not have the ability to vote on their own behalf, however, individual members have the opportunity to either form, or join a CSLA Chapter which allows a group of individual members to have a single collective vote.

Organizational Membership

To qualify for full membership with CSLA, an applicant must:

- be a non-profit organization registered in British Columbia or

- a non-profit organization registered in Canada and working in British Columbia, or

- be an organization, union, group, coalition, institution or private sector enterprise or

- a registered Chapter of CSLA with a minimum of 3 CSLA individual chapter members

Further, the applicant must also:

- Adhere to CSLA’s constitution, mission statement and bylaws

- Have been an incorporated society for at least one year

- Provide representation to and participation in CSLA activities as appropriate and when necessary

- Pay an annual membership fee, based on a scale that accounts for the organization’s annual budget

* Organizational Revenue for Previous Fiscal Year = Annual Fee

- $0 -$50,000 = $500

- $50,000 and up = $1,000

Chapter Membership

A Chapter will have at least three individual members as well as organizational members in good standing¹ with CSLA. The Chapter members will work together on a regular basis to participate in CSLA initiatives, programming and services.

CSLA’s head office provides the following types of support for Chapters:

- Review and make decisions on approval of a Chapter application in a timely manner

- One time Chapter start-up fund (for each membership collected by the Chapter in the first year, CSLA will give back 110 per cent of the membership contribution to the Chapter in the first year)

- Voting rights at CSLA AGM by one chapter representative.

¹A member in good standing is defined as a member who has paid for CSLA membership prior to the AGM.

Services

We provide a range of services that benefit the economy, society and environment to enable stakeholders to implement their sustainability goals.

Sustainability Consulting

We assist a diverse spectrum of educational institutions, corporate sectors and government entities in assessing their carbon impact, increasing employee awareness, developing a zero carbon strategy, and deliver programs that enhance stakeholder engagement to achieve ESG targets.

Carbon Offsetting

We offer a wide range of unique and innovating carbon offsetting programs, encompassing corporates, educational institutions and communities, directed towards mitigating biodiversity loss, pollution and climate change impacts in the world’s most vulnerable communities.

Sponsor Program

Your donation supports various programs and initiatives, which assists in the creation of local circular economies while fostering environmental stewardship.

Trainings & Academies

Our award winning “Sustainability Academy” delivers tailored content that leverages innovative communication methods and STEM learning to effectively engage all audiences and age groups. These are delivered in-person, virtual and/or in hybrid mode.
